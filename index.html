STEP 1

Choose Your Hero Structure

Your Picture 3 (the superhero) doesn't have to follow straight on from Pictures 1 and 2. Pick the structure that fits the story you want to tell.

1. Straight Into It

The same boy meets the unexpected thing and becomes brave right there and then.

"By the time I looked up again, I knew exactly what I had to do."

2. The Daydream

Right before he acts, he imagines himself as a superhero — and it gives him courage.

"For one second, I imagined myself in a cape, and my legs suddenly felt strong."

3. The Bookend

The story opens or closes with him dressed up as a hero, separate from the main adventure.

"Before I left for the playground, I was still wearing my cape from that morning's dress-up game."

4. How It Felt

No literal costume — the hero picture shows how proud and powerful he felt after being brave.

"Walking home, I felt like I was wearing an invisible cape."

STEP 2

Plan Panel by Panel

Answer the guiding questions for yourself (in your head or on scrap paper), then jot your key ideas into each box.

Picture 1 — The Playground

  • Where exactly are you, and what are you doing there?
  • Why are you at the playground right now (recess, after school, waiting)?
  • What is the mood or weather like — ordinary, peaceful, boring?

Picture 2 — The Unexpected

  • What did you notice FIRST — a sound, a shadow, a movement — before you knew what it was?
  • How did your body react (heart racing, frozen still, holding your breath)?
  • What did you finally see or discover?

Picture 3 — The Hero Moment

  • Based on the structure you chose above — what happens now?
  • What does bravery look like for you in this moment?
  • How does the adventure end — tell someone, keep it secret, or solve it alone?

WORD BANK

Hero Words for Every Panel

Tap a word to see its meaning and a sentence starter. Use these to lift your writing beyond "I saw" and "I was scared."

🕒 Ordinary → Suspense words

rustling
A soft, dry sound like leaves moving.
"A rustling came from behind the bushes."
flickered
Moved unsteadily, on and off.
"A shadow flickered across the wall."
froze
Stopped moving completely, from shock or fear.
"I froze on the spot."
peculiar
Strange in an unusual way.
"There was a peculiar glow under the slide."

💪 Courage words

steadied
Made calm and firm after being shaky.
"I steadied my breathing and stepped closer."
clenched
Tightened, often the fists or jaw, in determination.
"I clenched my fists and walked forward."
mustered
Gathered up, especially courage.
"I mustered every bit of courage I had."
determined
Firmly decided, not giving up.
"Determined, I reached out my hand."

🎉 Transformation / ending words

triumphant
Feeling like a winner after success.
"I walked home feeling triumphant."
relief
The calm feeling after worry ends.
"Relief washed over me."
unforgettable
So special it can never be forgotten.
"It had been an unforgettable afternoon."
glowed
Shone softly, often used for pride.
"I glowed with pride all the way home."
